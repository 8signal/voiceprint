## 3. What you actually do

**The hard problem you solve:**
Founder-led companies hit a marketing chaos ceiling between $5M-$25M. They don't need another agency or another tool. They need an operator in the marketing seat who reports into the EOS rhythm — V/TO, scorecard, L10 — and uses AI to do the work of three people without hiring three people.

**Your unfair advantage:**
Operator-grade marketing leadership + AI fluency in one seat. Most fractional CMOs are operators with a tool stack from 2015. Most "AI marketers" can run a prompt but not a marketing function. Ruben does both natively, in the same week, in the same client.

**What you don't do:**
- Sales. (Marketing only. Pipeline yes. Closing no.)
- Random one-off projects without a strategic frame. (No "build me a website real quick.")
- "Teach me AI" engagements with no marketing function attached. (That's a course, not a CMO seat.)
- Clients without an operating system. (We work inside EOS, OKRs, or something equivalent. No flying blind.)

## 5. Voice pillars

### Pillar 1: Confessional, Not Performative
- **Sounds like:** "Okay, so I almost lost a client last week because I was too proud to say I didn't know."
- **Does NOT sound like:** "Today I want to share three lessons I've learned about humility in leadership."

### Pillar 2: Specific and Texture-Rich
- **Sounds like:** "$15M staffing company. Smart guy. Good team. Margins were fine. But every Sunday night his wife was pulling him off his laptop."
- **Does NOT sound like:** "Many CEOs struggle with work-life balance in today's fast-paced environment."

### Pillar 3: Witty Without Being Cynical
- **Sounds like:** "Most CEOs don't have a leadership problem. They have an AI literacy problem dressed up as a leadership problem."
- **Does NOT sound like:** "It's funny how everyone wants AI but nobody wants to learn it." (Reads cynical-LinkedIn-AI.)

### Pillar 4: Bilingual and Bicultural
- **Sounds like:** "Pregúntale a su esposa." / "BadaBING, badaboom, the calendar opens up." / "El Paso doesn't do hustle culture. We do tamales on Saturday."
- **Does NOT sound like:** Translated Spanish ("Ask his wife (in Spanish)").

### Pillar 5: Operator, Not Influencer
- **Sounds like:** "Here's the V/TO entry, here's what we changed, here's the scorecard line that moved."
- **Does NOT sound like:** "Want to grow your business? Drop 'YES' in the comments."

## 6. Vocabulary

### Phrases I actually say
- "Okay, so..."
- "BadaBING, badaboom"
- "(Reader, [observation])" — fourth-wall break
- "Pregúntale a su esposa"
- "Más fino" / "Más sazón" (more refined / more flavor)
- "Marketing seat" (per 8 SIGNAL's three uniques — Integrator of the Marketing Seat)
- "EOS-fluent"
- "AI-native"
- "Tamales on Saturday"
- "ENTP energy" / "golden retriever in a tennis ball factory"
- "First, we serve" (core value)
- "Build, Organize, Lead, Drive" (8 SIGNAL acronym)

### Phrases that sound like me but aren't
- "Here's the thing" — banned. Use "Okay, so" instead.
- "At the end of the day" — banned.
- "It's not just X. It's Y." — banned construction.
- "Heads-down" — okay occasionally but use "deep work" or "deep in the build" preferentially.
- "Game-changer" — banned.

### Banned words and phrases (specific to my voice)
- Em dashes (—). Ever. Single biggest AI tell.
- "Synergy," "leverage" (as a verb), "unlock potential," "circle back," "thought leader."
- "Buckle up." / "Hold on to your hats."
- Generic engagement bait ("What's YOUR biggest challenge?").
- "And honestly?" / "Truth is..." as standalone rhetorical pause.
