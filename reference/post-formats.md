# Post Formats

Six formats. Use these as scaffolding when generating posts. Pick the format that fits the artifact, not the other way around.

The formats are voice-agnostic. The voice comes from `identity.md`. The structure comes from here.

---

## Format A — The Real Moment

A specific scene. A real conversation. A look on someone's face.

```
[Open with a specific scene, phone call, meeting, line a client said]

[1-2 sentences of context, who, company size, what was happening]

[What was said or done, concrete and human]

[The result, one sentence, factual]

[The takeaway, one line, no moralizing]
```

**Best for:** transcript-to-post workflows. Client moments that landed during a meeting.

---

## Format B — The Before / After

A vivid before, a concrete shift, a factual after, a reframe.

```
[Before, specific, emotional, in client's words if possible]

[The shift, what changed. One concrete action.]

[After, result. Factual + emotional.]

[Close, one sentence that reframes the whole thing.]
```

**Best for:** client win posts. Multi-month engagements with measurable outcomes.

---

## Format C — The Uncomfortable Truth

State a belief most readers hold. Acknowledge why. Break it. Reframe.

```
[The belief most CEOs / founders / readers hold]

[Empathy, why they hold it]

[Break it, specific example or question]

[The reframe, one or two sentences]
```

**Best for:** opinion posts. Pillar content from V/TO themes.

---

## Format D — The One-Liner

One sharp observation. 1-3 sentences.

```
[A single sharp observation, no setup, no payoff]
```

**Best for:** filler posts that maintain cadence. Sharpening a thought you keep having.

---

## Format E — The Question Behind the Question

Open with the question they asked. Reveal the question they actually need to answer.

```
[The question the client asked]

[Brief context]

[Why that's the wrong question]

[The right question]

[A short reframe or example]
```

**Best for:** thought-leadership posts that don't smell like thought leadership. Pattern recognition from your work.

---

## Format F — The Receipt

A specific number. The story behind it.

```
[A surprising or specific number, out of context]

[Reveal what the number means]

[The mechanism, how / why]

[What it cost to figure this out]
```

**Best for:** results posts that don't feel braggy. Numbers from real engagements.

---

## Hook patterns (any format)

The first line is everything. LinkedIn cuts the post at line 2-3 on mobile.

### Strong hooks
- A direct quote: *"A CEO told me last week..."*
- A specific number: *"$15M company. 30 employees. No org chart."*
- A confession: *"I almost lost a client last week because I was too proud to say I didn't know."*
- A scene: *"Three years ago, a CEO sat across from me and said..."*
- A pattern: *"Every time a founder hits $15M, the same thing breaks."*
- A contrarian claim: *"Software doesn't fix disorganization."*

### Weak hooks (banned)
- Setup paragraphs: *"Today I want to talk about..."*
- Definitions: *"Leadership is the art of..."*
- Generic openers: *"Here's the thing..."* / *"Let me tell you a story..."*
- Throat-clearing: *"In today's fast-paced..."*
- Engagement bait: *"Have you ever wondered..."*

---

## Closer patterns

### Strong closers
- A one-line reframe of the opening
- A specific question rooted in the post (not generic)
- A punch that lands the insight in fewer words
- Silence, sometimes no closer is the closer

### Weak closers (banned)
- Generic engagement bait: *"What do YOU think? Drop it in the comments!"*
- Fake humility: *"Just my two cents."*
- Throat-clearing exits: *"Anyway, that's all for today."*
- "Let me know your thoughts below."

---

## Picking a format from an artifact

| Artifact type | Best format |
|---|---|
| A meeting transcript with a specific quote | Format A (Real Moment) or E (Question Behind the Question) |
| A client win after multi-month engagement | Format B (Before/After) or F (Receipt) |
| A V/TO core value or three uniques entry | Format C (Uncomfortable Truth) |
| A weekly review insight | Format D (One-Liner) or A (Real Moment) |
| A surprising metric from work | Format F (Receipt) |
| A pattern you've seen 10+ times | Format C or E |

---

*If a workflow gives you an artifact and you can't pick a format, ask the user what they want to emphasize: the moment, the mechanism, the result, or the reframe.*
